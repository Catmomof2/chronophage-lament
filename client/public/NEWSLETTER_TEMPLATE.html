New Beginnings and Temporal Reflections

As we step into the new year, I find myself reflecting on the nature of time itself—a theme that has woven through every page of The Chronophage's Lament. This month has been filled with quiet mornings at my desk, watching the Washington rain create patterns on the window, each drop a tiny universe of possibility.

I'm thrilled to share that I've begun outlining my next project, a companion piece that explores the spaces between moments. While The Chronophage's Lament followed Kai's journey through a surreal landscape, this new work will delve into the consciousness of time itself. What does it mean to exist in the eternal now? How do we reconcile memory with possibility?

The response to The Chronophage's Lament has been overwhelming. Your messages about which chapters resonated most, which passages you've returned to, and how the story has influenced your own perception of time—these connections fuel my creative spirit more than any cup of coffee ever could.

Inside My Creative Process

Many of you have asked about my writing routine, so I thought I'd pull back the curtain a bit. My typical day begins around 5:30 AM, before the world fully wakes. There's something magical about those pre-dawn hours when the boundary between dreams and reality feels thin.

My three cats—Luna, Kafka, and Dali—are my constant companions during these early sessions. Luna insists on sitting directly on my keyboard at least once per hour, which I've come to interpret as her editorial feedback. Kafka prefers to knock pens off my desk with philosophical precision, while Dali simply stares at the wall, perhaps seeing dimensions I cannot.

I write in longhand first, using fountain pens and notebooks. There's a tactile connection between hand and page that helps me access deeper layers of thought. Only after a scene feels complete do I transfer it to the computer, often discovering new nuances in the process of transcription. This double-writing technique slows me down, but it also ensures that every word earns its place.

The surrealist elements in my work often emerge from this liminal state between sleep and waking. I keep a dream journal beside my bed, and many of the stranger images in The Chronophage's Lament—the melting clock creature, the river of moments, the Chronitect's tower—originated in those midnight visions.

Exclusive Preview

From the Upcoming Work: 'The Stillness Between Seconds'

The Keeper of Pauses lived in the space between heartbeats. Not metaphorically, but literally—in that infinitesimal gap where the world holds its breath, where cause has not yet become effect, where the universe contemplates its next move.

She had no form, not exactly. To perceive her was to experience a moment of profound hesitation, as if reality itself had second thoughts. Some described her as a shimmer, others as an absence, still others as the feeling of a word on the tip of your tongue that you can never quite remember.

"Every pause is a choice," she would say to those rare souls who stumbled into her domain. "Every silence is a universe of unspoken possibilities. You rush through your moments like they're obstacles to overcome, never realizing that the spaces between are where true freedom lives."

In her realm, time didn't flow—it pooled. Past and future existed simultaneously, separated only by the thinnest membrane of now. And in that eternal present, anything could happen, or nothing at all...

Thank you, truly, for your continued support. Every donation, every kind word, every moment you spend with my work—it all matters more than I can express. You're not just supporting an author; you're supporting a vision of literature that embraces the strange, the philosophical, the beautifully inexplicable.

This month, your contributions helped me invest in new writing software, replenish my seemingly endless supply of notebooks, and yes, keep the kitties in premium treats. But more importantly, your support gives me the freedom to take creative risks, to explore ideas that might not fit conventional molds, to write stories that challenge and inspire.

As we move through this new year together, I'm excited to share more of my journey with you. There will be new stories, deeper explorations, and perhaps a few surprises along the way. Thank you for walking this path with me.

— Amanda
